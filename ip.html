What is Intellectual Property?

Intellectual property (IP) includes all student creations that have taken tangible form — from inventions and software to research papers and datasets. IP law gives the creator the right to control, use, and profit from their work.

💡

Patents

Protect inventions and novel processes. If you invent something at UVA using university resources, the university may have a claim.

©️

Copyright

Automatically protects original creative works — code, writing, art, research — from the moment of creation. No registration needed.

™️

Trade Secrets

Confidential business/research information with commercial value. Sharing trade secrets without authorization can have serious legal consequences.

Ownership & Rights

Most engineering students are unaware that inventions created using university resources legally belong to the university, not the student. This is a crucial fact to understand before you build anything.

Key Principle If your project uses UVA labs, equipment, funding, or faculty guidance, UVA likely has rights to that invention. If it's a purely personal project on your own time with your own resources, you retain full ownership.

UVA Policy Highlights

  • Inventions made with university support or resources are typically owned by UVA
  • UVA's Licensing & Ventures Group manages commercialization of faculty and student inventions
  • Students can negotiate revenue-sharing agreements when their inventions are commercialized
  • Research data generated at UVA may have data-sharing obligations under grant agreements
  • Thesis and dissertation work is generally owned by the student but UVA retains a license to use it

Collaboration Rules

Collaborating on projects without clear written agreements is a common source of IP disputes. Before you start building something with others, define these in writing:

  • Ownership split — who owns what percentage of the final product?
  • Profit division — how are revenues or licensing fees divided?
  • Exit conditions — what happens if one collaborator leaves?
  • Contribution tracking — how is each person's contribution documented?
💼 Practical Tip Even informal collaborations — classmates building a project together — benefit from a simple written agreement. It avoids conflict and protects everyone's contribution.

NDAs & Confidentiality

Non-disclosure agreements (NDAs) are legally binding contracts requiring you to keep certain information confidential. You may be asked to sign one when interning, working in a lab, or collaborating with a company.

⚠ Read Before You Sign Never sign an NDA without understanding what information you're agreeing to protect, for how long, and what the penalties for breach are. When in doubt, consult UVA's Student Legal Services.
